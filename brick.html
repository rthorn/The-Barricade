The Barricade is a siege defense game inspired by Les Misérables' depiction of the Paris Uprising of 1832. Your goal is to defend the barricade against waves of attacks by the French military until you gain enough

Hope

for a full-fledged revolution.

Gameplay

Waves of French soldiers are attacking your barricade. Your barricade is defended by characters known as Amis and their supplies of

Ammo

,

Food

, and

Hope

. To win, you must either purchase the RÉVOLUTION! upgrade for 10,000

Hope

or survive 40 Waves and recruit Victor Hugo for 2000

Hope

. If any single wall of your barricade falls, it's game over.

Each Wave is split into three phases:

Prepare

,

Fight!

, and

Recover

. While enemies will only attack the central Chanvrerie barricade at first, they will eventually begin to attack the smaller Mondetour barricade on the left, and even a new Precheurs barricade that will need to be built on the right.

Prepare

Prepare

for the upcoming battle by placing Amis on the central barricade walls or in the Corinthe building. Amis can shoot the enemy from either location, however, a wall without any Amis protecting it will fall much faster. Once enemies begin attacking the side barricades, you will need to place Amis there are well, while Amis in buildings can attack enemies on either side.

Amis on the barricades are directly in the line of fire, but the more Amis on a single wall, the less damage any one Ami on that wall will take. The higher the wall, the more Amis can fit on it. Higher walls will also better shield Amis from enemy fire.

Fight!

The French military appears in the

Fight!

phase to exchange bullets with your Amis. You must either survive until the day is over, or kill all of your enemies first. You earn increasing

Hope

every time you survive a Wave, and 2x

Hope

if you finish early. You will lose

Hope

for any Ami that dies.

Amis in buildings cannot move during the battle, but those on the barricade or not currently fighting will be able to move around freely. Both your Amis and enemies will miss more frequently the lower their health is. In addition, your Amis will not be able to shoot if you run out of

Ammo

.

Recover

After the battle you have a chance to

Recover

. Amis can get ready for the upcoming Wave in many ways: looting ammo and food, building up the barricade, scouting for information, healing their wounds, or just hanging out and drinking.
  • Drinking gains between 5-10

    Hope

    per Ami.
  • Looting gains between 5-10

    Food

    or

    Ammo

    per Ami.

  • Food

    can be used to feed Amis once each during recovery, giving them back 25 health. If you are out of

    Food

    , Amis cannot gain

    Hope

    from drinking.
  • Building the barricade walls increases their height, staving off their destruction.
  • Scouting will give information of the upcoming attack, but has a 20% risk of death. Scouting will give you up to 3 Wave notice of a new barricade being attacked for the first time.
  • Resting in the Corinthe building will heal 40% of an Ami's health and give a temporary 20% damage boost for being well-rested.

Ammo

,

Food

, and

Hope

can also be used to purchase upgrades, including damage and health buffs, improvements to the barricade and buildings, and the ability to recruit Citizens and other unique Amis to your cause during the

Recover

phase.

A small Precheurs barricade will be automatically built immediately before being attacked from the right for the first time. After the Precheurs barricade is built, your Amis are surrounded and all looting will come with a 20% risk of death.

Controls

Hold

Space

during the

Fight!

phase to increase the speed by 3x. Release

Space

to go back to normal speed.

Shift

-drag a stack of Citizens to drag the entire stack.

Shift-Ctrl

-drag a stack of Citizens on to an active barricade wall to evenly distribute the Citizens across all active barricade walls.

Enemies

New enemy types will appear as you progress through the game.

Soldiers

Simple Soldiers will attack from the very beginning. They do a small amount of damage to the barricades and the Amis on them, but are unable to target Amis in buildings.

Snipers

Snipers can shoot any Ami, including those in adjacent buildings, and do a much larger amount of damage per shot but shoot less frequently. Amis hit by a Sniper will briefly flash yellow as an indicator.

Cannons

Cannons will infrequently knock all Amis off a wall of the barricade, doing significant damage to those Amis and leaving the wall unprotected until you replace them.

Javert

Javert is a unique enemy who does not attack during the

Fight!

phase with the other enemies. When recruiting Citizens, it's possible for Javert to sneak in. He will be indistinguishable from other Citizens, but will sabotage any activity he is a part of and will not defend the barricade or be attacked by enemies. He can be dismissed from the barricade, gaining you 500

Hope

, but if dismissed, he may return later.

Amis

You start the game with 8 unique, named Amis. As the game continues, you can recruit generic Citizens as well as new unique Amis. Each unique Ami has a special ability that can be upgraded to become more powerful.

Citizens

Citizens start with much lower health and damage than unique Amis, but all Citizens can be made stronger through upgrades. Individual Citizens can be taught the special abilities of one or more unique Amis after the training upgrade has been purchased. Identical Citizens will stack in some contexts; see Controls for some ways to make moving stacks easier.

There is a limit to the number of Citizens that can be recruited, which you can increase through upgrades. When recruiting new Citizens, watch out for Javert.

Unique Amis

Each unique Ami can be individually upgraded to improve their health, damage, or special ability. Each special ability starts at level 1 and can be upgraded 3 times, up to level 4. Trained citizens will receive the ability at the same level as the unique Ami at the time of training. Citizens cannot train other Citizens, so if a unique Ami dies or is dismissed, it will no longer be possible to train new Citizens in their ability.

Enjolras

Enjolras regains a certain percentage of lost health at the end of a Wave. This ability cannot increase health above 100%. Amis healed to full health by this ability do count as being at full health at the end of the Wave for the Missed Me achievement.
  1. Regains 25% health at end of wave
  2. Regains 50% health at end of wave
  3. Regains 75% health at end of wave
  4. Regains all health at end of wave

Bahorel

Bahorel has a 5% chance of making a critical hit that does extra damage. The extra damage amount is multiplied by the base damage done by the Ami, so higher base damage will increase the power of the critical hit even further.
  1. 5% chance to do 1.5x damage
  2. 5% chance to do 2x damage
  3. 5% chance to do 3x damage
  4. 5% chance to do 5x damage

Bossuet

Bossuet is terribly unlucky and attracts enemy bullets. Enemies are more likely to target him than any other individual Ami, making him and, if he is on a barricade, the wall he is on take more damage. Other Amis and barricade walls take less damage as a result.
  1. 2x as likely to be attacked
  2. 4x as likely to be attacked
  3. 10x as likely to be attacked
  4. 25x as likely to be attacked

Combeferre

Combeferre finds extra

Ammo

when looting.
  1. Finds 1.5x ammo
  2. Finds 2x ammo
  3. Finds 3x ammo
  4. Finds 5x ammo

Courfeyrac

When Courfeyrac rests during the

Recover

phase, he improves the healing and temporary damage boost all Amis get from resting. If multiple Amis with this ability rest at the same time, their abilities stack. Amis cannot be healed above 100% health, but can receive a temporary damage boost of any amount.
  1. When resting, all resting gain 5% more health and damage boost
  2. When resting, all resting gain 10% more health and damage boost
  3. When resting, all resting gain 15% more health and damage boost
  4. When resting, all resting gain 20% more health and damage boost

Feuilly

Feuilly does extra build on the barricade walls.
  1. Does 1.5x build on barricade
  2. Does 2x build on barricade
  3. Does 3x build on barricade
  4. Does 5x build on barricade

Joly

Joly finds extra

Food

when looting.
  1. Finds 1.5x food
  2. Finds 2x food
  3. Finds 3x food
  4. Finds 5x food

Prouvaire

Prouvaire's heroism in the face of death inspires the other Amis. When Prouvaire dies, instead of losing

Hope

,

Hope

is gained.
  1. Boosts hope by 25 at death
  2. Boosts hope by 50 at death
  3. Boosts hope by 75 at death
  4. Boosts hope by 100 at death
